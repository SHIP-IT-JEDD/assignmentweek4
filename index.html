The Propensity Clock

After A. Quetelet's Treatise on Man (1835)

What You're About to See

In 1835, Belgian statistician Adolphe Quetelet analyzed thousands of people and discovered something disturbing: your age predicts your behavior.

He measured six key "propensities" across different ages—crime, marriage, madness, strength, creativity, and death. Each one follows a predictable curve.

His radical claim: These patterns prove we have less free will than we think. Society and age determine who we are, not our choices.

Below, you'll drag a slider through the ages of life. Watch how all six propensities shift as you age. Is Quetelet right? Are we just predictable clockwork?
